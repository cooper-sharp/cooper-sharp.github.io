Hello, I'm Cooper!

I'm a Laboratory Manager at the Temple University Neuroeconomics Lab, where I study reward processing, decision-making, and social cognition across the adult lifespan. I'm also pursuing an M.S. in Business Analytics at Temple.

My research focuses on gambling behavior, financial exploitation risk, and fMRI methodology. I'm a consistent contributor to open science—maintaining datasets on OpenNeuro and pre-registrations on OSF.

Current Work

I manage data collection and analysis for a longitudinal NIH-funded study (R01-067011) investigating social reward processing in over 300 participants aged 21–90. This includes designing fMRI preprocessing pipelines, administering neuropsychological batteries, and mentoring undergraduate researchers on their independent projects.

I recently designed a 60-subject neuroimaging study examining neural differences in reward response, action inhibition, and betting behavior between gamblers and non-gamblers with high reward sensitivity. Data collections and analyses for this project are ongoing and expected to complete in Fall 2026.
