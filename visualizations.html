Where Is Our Data From?

Our studies drew from a geographically diverse sample across the United States. Participants were recruited from all 50 states plus the District of Columbia and Puerto Rico, with concentrations in major population centers.

  • Distribution — CA, NY, TX, and FL, represent 32% of the sample -- accurate to the US population.
  • Legal Landscape — Our sample provides a diverse representation of geographic legality for retail and online gambling.
Key takeaway: Our sample is closely representative of the US population distribution.

Who Is in the Dataset?

Understanding the demographic composition of our sample is essential for interpreting findings and assessing generalizability. The total sample is comprised of three individual studies.

  • Age distribution — Ages are clustred due to sample targeting in the individual studies.
  • Gender breakdown — Gender is distributed consistent with the general US population.
  • Race composition — The studies targeted a diverse range of individuals.
  • Education levels — Most participants finished high school, with a large portion obtaining a college education.
  • Income distribution — Most of the sample earns less than the median US household income ($83,730; U.S. Census Bureau, September 2025)
Key takeaway: The sample is diverse, yet representative. However, self-reported income is lower than expected.

Where Is Problem Gambling Most Common?

Problem gambling rates vary significantly by state. This analysis identifies geographic hotspots where intervention efforts may be most needed.

  • Highest rate state — Nearly half (42.55%) of respondents from Alabama reported potential problem gambling.
  • Regional patterns — The US Southeast has 3 of the top 5 rates of problem gambling in our sample.
  • Comparison to national — Though national estimates are increasing, our sample revealed a far greater prevelance of problem gambling. We assume this is due to the bias of hosting via the internet.

Online vs. Casino: Which Dominates?

The landscape of gambling is shifting from traditional casinos to online platforms. This analysis compares the frequency of online and casino gambling across states.

  • Overall trend — Online gambling is much more popular than casino gambling, potentially revealing an effect of convenience.
  • Top online states — VA, LA, and LI, preferred online or virtual gambling, all averaging >6 days per month.
  • Top casino states — AZ, CT, and IN, preferred physical or casino gambling, all averaging >2 days per month.
